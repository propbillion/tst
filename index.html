Teaching Method

Concepts before
shortcuts.

Traditional classes rely on the teacher speaking and the student listening. At CMI, students do more than listen — they visualise, discuss, and engage in the higher-order work of analysis and evaluation.

01

Logical & Scientific Approach

Students are exposed to fundamental concepts under a clear, structured framework. The application of each concept is built through illustrations of stepwise increasing difficulty — from grasp to mastery.

02

Three Lectures Per Day

Each lecture is one hour long. During school breaks and summer vacations, frequency increases to maintain academic continuity. Concept building happens through theory, illustration, and derivation.

03

Comprehensive Study Material

Notes, assignments, Daily Practice Problems (DPPs), test papers, and a question bank — all developed in-house through continuous research and refinement, exclusive to enrolled students.

04

Visual-Based Instruction

Hi-tech teaching aids work alongside chalk and talk. Visual instruction provides a helpful platform for learning complex concepts with ease, leaving a lasting impact on student understanding.

05

Periodic Batch Reshuffling

Students are reshuffled across batches periodically. This inculcates a healthy competitive spirit and ensures every student is placed in an environment matched to their current pace.

06

One-Stop Academic Solution

Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics and Biology — all covered within the classroom programme. A student does not need to coordinate multiple coaching arrangements for their academic needs.

Assessment Method

In-depth evaluation.
Three layers.

Actual evaluation requires more than one form of testing. CMI's assessment system combines formal, informal, and self-assessment — each addressing a different dimension of learning.

Formal

Periodic & surprise tests

Structured testing to measure progress and inform the teacher whether a student is ready for the next level. Both objective and subjective formats. Cumulative across topics.

Informal

Class observation & DPPs

Teachers observe student response in class, review homework, and evaluate Daily Practice Problems. This identifies students who need additional support before it shows in formal tests.

Self-Assessment

Student-led reflection

Students assess what they have learned and identify where they need to improve. This builds metacognition — the ability to direct one's own learning, which is essential for entrance preparation.

Student Support

Personal attention.
Continuous guidance.

Personalised attention from student-friendly management, staff, and faculty — so that no student remains a face in the crowd.

Special Doubt-Clearing Sessions

Dedicated sessions outside regular classes. Doubts are addressed one-on-one or in small groups, ensuring no concept is left unresolved before the next topic begins.

Extended Academic Support

Full academic support continues outside scheduled classes through regular problem-solving counters and individual counselling sessions.

Indiscriminate Teaching

Teaching does not vary based on past performance. Every student receives the same quality of instruction and faculty time, with the goal of exploring full potential.

Parent–Teacher Meetings

Held on a regular basis. Parents receive direct feedback on academic progress, attendance patterns, and areas where home support can reinforce classroom work.

Daily Progress Monitoring

Daily progress monitoring, regular tests, and structured performance analysis. Feedback flows continuously between faculty, student, and parent.

Caring Administration

The administration acts as a mentor from the day of admission to course completion — coordinating academic and non-academic activities to ensure overall effectiveness.
